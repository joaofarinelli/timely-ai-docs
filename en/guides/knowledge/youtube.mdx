With the YouTube integration, you can turn any video into searchable knowledge for your agent. Timely.ai automatically extracts the video transcript, processes the text into chunks, and generates the embeddings — without requiring you to transcribe anything manually.

## How It Works

The pipeline follows these steps internally:

<Steps>
  <Step title="URL validation">
    You provide the video URL. The system validates that it is a valid YouTube link and fetches the public video metadata (title, channel, thumbnail).
  </Step>
  <Step title="User confirmation">
    The video data is displayed for confirmation before processing begins — title, channel, and thumbnail — to ensure it is the correct video.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Transcript extraction">
    The `youtube-processor` fetches the automatic transcript or the video's subtitles via the YouTube API.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Chunking and embeddings">
    The transcript text is split into chunks (following the same document pipeline) and vectorized by OpenAI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Made available in the base">
    The chunks become immediately available for semantic search by the agent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding a Video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the YouTube section">
    In the agent, go to **Knowledge Base > YouTube**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the video URL">
    Enter the full URL of the YouTube video. Accepted formats:
    - `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIDEO_ID`
    - `https://youtu.be/VIDEO_ID`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the video">
    Verify the displayed title and channel. Click **Confirm and process**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for processing">
    The status changes to `processing` and then to `success` when complete. Processing time varies depending on the video's duration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The video must have a publicly available transcript or subtitle track. Videos without automatic or manual captions enabled cannot be processed.
</Note>

## Important Limitations

| Limitation | Detail |
|---|---|
| Maximum indexed characters | 1,028 characters per chunk (maximum size configured in the processor) |
| Language support | Depends on the subtitles available on YouTube |
| Private videos | Not supported — public videos only |
| Live streams | Live transcripts may have reduced quality |

## Managing Indexed Videos

The indexed video list displays the following for each item:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Thumbnail | Video thumbnail |
| Title | Video title on YouTube |
| Channel | Name of the channel that published the video |
| Total chunks | Number of excerpts generated from the transcript |
| Indexing date | When the video was processed |

To remove a video from the base, click the actions menu and select **Delete**. All chunks and embeddings for that video are deleted.

<Warning>
  Deleting an indexed video permanently removes all associated chunks. The agent loses access to that video's content immediately after deletion.
</Warning>

## Recommended Use Cases

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Training and tutorials" icon="graduation-cap">
    Index onboarding or internal training videos so the agent can answer questions about the content.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Webinars and talks" icon="video">
    Make knowledge shared in recorded events accessible without manually transcribing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Product demonstrations" icon="play">
    Product demo videos allow the agent to explain specific features based on what was said in the video.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Video FAQ" icon="circle-question">
    If your team recorded answers to frequently asked questions on video, index them and the agent will know what was explained.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Videos with manual captions (created by humans) produce transcripts of far superior quality compared to automatic captions generated by YouTube. Prefer videos with manual captions for critical topics.
</Tip>
