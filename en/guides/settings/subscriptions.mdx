## Available plans

Timely.ai offers three subscription plans with monthly or annual billing. All include access to AI agents, service channels, squads, and monthly AI credits:

| Plan | Description |
|---|---|
| **Starter** | For solo professionals and small businesses just getting started with service automation. Includes a single user with full access and 1,500 monthly credits. |
| **Professional** | For growing companies that need more volume, multiple users, and advanced features such as smart broadcasts, automatic follow-ups, Tyme CRM, and voice synthesis. Includes 7,500 monthly credits. |
| **Enterprise** | For large-scale operations with high service volume. Unlimited executions, 50,000 monthly credits, unlimited TTS, voice cloning, and premium support with a 24-hour SLA. |

<Note>
  Annual plans are discounted compared to the monthly equivalent. The amount is charged in a single installment at the start of the annual cycle, and the full period's credits (12 months) are made available at once — not renewed month by month.
</Note>

## Understanding each limit

### Timely.ai AI credits

Credits are the unit of AI processing consumption on the platform. Each message processed by an agent consumes credits according to the selected LLM model:

| Model | Credits per message |
|---|---|
| Kimi K2.5 (OpenRouter) | 1 |
| Gemini 2.5 Flash (Google) | 2 |
| Claude Haiku 4.5 (Anthropic) | 3 |
| GPT-5.1 (OpenAI) | 4 |
| Claude Sonnet 4.6 (Anthropic) | 4 |
| Gemini 2.5 Pro (Google) | 4 |
| GPT-5.2 (OpenAI) | 5 |
| Gemini 3.0 / 3.1 Pro Preview (Google) | 6 |
| Claude Opus 4.6 (Anthropic) | 20 |

The cost per message varies with context length and the number of tool calls — the table above reflects the base cost per simple interaction. Renewable credits are reset at the start of each cycle. Unused credits do not carry over to the next period.

<Warning>
  Agents configured with own providers (customer API key) do not consume Timely.ai credits — the cost is billed directly to the provider account. Plan credits apply only to agents using the platform's managed credentials.
</Warning>

### Agent executions

An execution is counted each time an agent is triggered by an incoming message — whether from a service channel or a Workers session. Handoffs within a squad count as separate executions for each agent involved:

| Plan | Monthly executions |
|---|---|
| Starter | 1,000 |
| Professional | 5,000 |
| Enterprise | Unlimited |

### Workflow executions

Workflows are automated flows triggered by events (message received, scheduled time, tag added, etc.). Each workflow trigger counts as one execution:

| Plan | Concurrent workflow executions |
|---|---|
| Starter | 3 |
| Professional | 10 |
| Enterprise | Unlimited |

<Note>
  Workflow executions are concurrent — the limit refers to the number of workflows running simultaneously, not the monthly total. Triggers that exceed the concurrency limit are queued and executed when a slot opens.
</Note>

### Members and seats

A seat is an active user with workspace access. Deactivated members do not count toward the limit:

| Plan | Members/seats |
|---|---|
| Starter | 1 |
| Professional | Unlimited |
| Enterprise | Unlimited |

<Note>
  The Starter plan is designed for individual use — a single user with full workspace access. For teams with two or more collaborators, Professional is the minimum recommended plan.
</Note>

## Managing your subscription

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings > Subscription">
    In the sidebar, click **Settings** and select **Subscription** (or **Billing**, depending on the interface version).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Change plan">
    On the current plan card, click **Change plan** to open the comparison modal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the cycle and desired plan">
    Select between **Monthly** and **Annual** billing at the top of the modal. Then choose the plan and click **Select**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the change">
    The confirmation modal displays the preview: amount to pay today, proportional credit from the current plan, and start date of the new cycle. Read and click **Confirm change**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **Upgrade:** applied immediately, with proportional billing for the remaining period. Access to new features unlocked right away. **Downgrade:** applied at the start of the next cycle. You keep the current plan until the end of the paid period.
</Note>

## Plan behavior

| Aspect | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Upgrade** | Immediate, with proportional credit from the current plan |
| **Downgrade** | Scheduled for the start of the next cycle |
| **Cancellation** | Access remains until the end of the paid period |
| **Reactivation** | Available until the end of the grace period after cancellation |

## Conclusion

Choosing the right plan directly impacts workspace operational capacity:

- Monitor credit and execution consumption on the **Usage** tab before deciding between upgrade and downgrade.
- Take the LLM model of your agents into account when projecting monthly consumption — models like Claude Opus 4.6 (20 credits/msg) exhaust the Starter balance in 75 messages.
- Annual plans offer a discount and make the full year's credits available at once — advantageous for workspaces with predictable volume.

<Note>
  Unused credits at the end of a monthly cycle are zeroed out. For workspaces with irregular consumption, the annual plan may result in underutilized balance. Evaluate your consumption history before migrating to the annual cycle.
</Note>
